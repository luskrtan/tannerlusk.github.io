Design Principles

Visual Hierarchy

Cabela's

The hierarchy of this website is 1) The camping image to announce the "Camping Classic" sale, 2) The "Shop Deals By Category" uses large text and two smaller images to then provide access to shopping categories, 3) The "Featured" section has large text but no images to then show other sales, and then 4) the account, shopping-cart, search bar section has the smallest text but also the logo to grab the viewer's attention last.

White Space

Dunkin' Donuts

This website uses a lot of white space to not only draw attention to the appealing image of the donut, but also, in contrast with the bright pinks and oranges fo the text and headers, serves to separate the different actions that can be performed on the website. Alignment

Rock Auto

The list of car brands is the only thing that's listed from top to bottom, while the different types of searches that can be performed are listed from left to right. This allows for two types of general searches to be made. If you know the car make and model but don't know the specific part, you can look at the top to bottom list. If you know the specific part, however, you can look at the left to right list to make a swifter search.
